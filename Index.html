About

Art is more than just art, it’s a bold fusion of vision, imagination, and style. We bring colors to life
through vibrant, expressive creations that speak to the soul and stand out from the ordinary. From digital
and photo portraits to custom merch and style focused visuals, every piece we make is primarly designed
to celebrate personality, culture, and creativity. Whether you want to wear it, frame it, or share it, VYX
turns your ideas into unforgettable visuals that feel personal and powerful.

Born from a love for all things bold and beautiful, VYX is where your ideas meet our artistic fire. We
collaborate with individuals, brands, and creatives who crave art that’s fresh, fearless, and full of flair.
From sleek logos and posters to splashy paint style designs, everything we create is made to tell stories,
spark emotions, and leave a lasting impression.

At VYX, we don’t just sell art — we create style with soul and experiences you can feel.

Let’s turn your vision into a masterpiece, one that looks as good as it feels.

NEWS & UPDATES

1. Wallpaper as Collectible Art

Artist-designed wallpaper is exploding as immersive, limited edition art objects, hand-painted and silkscreened installations by Laura Owens, Dominique Fung, Kerstin Brätsch, Kiriakos Tompolidis, Philip Wiegard, and more at Matthew Marks Gallery and Brooklyn’s Independent Art Fair.

Read more: Is Wallpaper the Art World’s Next Big Thing? (Town & Country)



2. Matthew Barney’s TACTICAL parallax at AIR Aspen

Provocateur Matthew Barney premieres a live “western” performance in a repurposed WWII drill hall fusing Redoubt (2018) and Secondary (2023) to explore American violence through football and wilderness at Aspen Art Museum’s AIR 2025 festival.

Read more: Matthew Barney: ‘I’m not interested in participating in consensus culture’ (The Guardian)



3. Andy Goldsworthy: Fifty Years at the RSA, Edinburgh

Over 200 works by the British land-artist, stone cairns, ice sculptures, sheepskin staircases, barbed-wire installations, reframe minimalism as nature’s geometry in the Royal Scottish Academy’s largest indoor survey ever.

Read more: Andy Goldsworthy: Fifty Years (National Galleries of Scotland)



4. AI Art’s “Builders” vs. “Breakers”

Peter Bauman’s essay in The Art Newspaper unpacks whether AI artists are constructive pioneers forging new aesthetics or disruptive forces upending authorship, ultimately showing they’re often both.

Read more: Why artificial intelligence artists can be seen as ‘builders’, ‘breakers’ or both at once (The Art Newspaper)



5. Museum of AI Art: Dataland (LA, late 2025)

Co-founded by Refik Anadol, Dataland will be the first museum dedicated entirely to AI art, 20,000 sq ft of data driven “paintings” and “sculptures” under one roof next to MOCA and The Broad.

Read more: World’s first AI art museum to explore ‘creative potential of machines’ in LA (The Guardian)
